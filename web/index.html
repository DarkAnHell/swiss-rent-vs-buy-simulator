Summary

Net Worth Over Time

▲ Higher is better

Total wealth per strategy — investments, pillar balances, and home equity minus mortgage. The main lens for comparing long-term outcomes.

Annual Cash Outflow

▼ Lower is better

Total cash spent per year: housing costs, taxes, living expenses. Shows when buying costs more or less than renting on a year-by-year basis.

Net Cash Flow

▲ Higher is better

Income minus all expenses. Negative values mean drawing down savings that year; positive values add to investments. Indicates years of financial stress.

Cash Liquidity

▲ Higher is better

Investable liquid assets (stocks/cash). Excludes locked pillars and home equity. The large drop at year 0 for buyers reflects the down payment.

Buy vs Rent Delta

▲ Higher is better — positive = buying leads

Net worth difference for each buy strategy minus renting. Positive = buying leads; negative = renting leads. Zero crossing is the financial breakeven year.

Annual Gap Change

▲ Higher is better — green = buying gaining ground

Year-over-year change in the buy/rent gap. Green bars mean buying gained ground that year; red bars mean renting gained ground.

Buy Win Share

▲ Higher is better — above 50% = buying wins majority

Percentage of simulated parameter combinations where buying beats renting. Above 50% means buying wins in the majority of scenarios.

Delta Histogram

Wider spread = more outcome uncertainty

Distribution of buy/rent net worth differences across all scenarios. Orange bars: buying won; blue bars: renting won. Use the slider to explore year by year.

Not financial advice — results are illustrative only. All calculations run entirely in your browser; no data is sent to or stored on any server.
